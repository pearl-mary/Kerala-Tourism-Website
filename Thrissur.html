Thrissur

About Thrissur

Thrissur is regarded as the cultural capital of Kerala, a beautiful coastal region of southwest India with a history deeply rooted in the spice trade. For an exercise in absolute serenity, schedule an overnight houseboat tour of the local backwaters. If you’re lucky enough to visit during the temple festival of Pooram, you’ll experience lively music performances, fireworks, shadow puppet shows and processionals of beautifully adorned elephants.

Places to visit in Thrissur

Shakthan Thampuran Palace,Thrissur

Sakthan Thampuran palace is situated in the heart of the Thrissur in Kerala named after Sakthan Thampuran, the king of Cochin. This is currently preserved as a musuem under the management of the Archaeological department. Apart from the museum there is a herbal garden in the area where the king Sakthan Thampuran was laid to rest.

Athirapilly and Vazhachal Falls chalakudy,Thrissur

Besides its famous backwaters, the South Indian state of Kerala is also known for some beautiful waterfalls set in the tranquil backdrop of the Western Ghats mountain ranges. Visit two of the most famous waterfalls of Kerala - the Athirapilly and the Vazhachal Falls. The dense forests of the region are also home to rich flora fauna.

Peechi Dam,Thrissur

This is a small dam, one of dozens in Kerala, but beautiful. Access (small fee payable) is down a wide path and across the causeway giving great views of the water spraying out to the river below, the generating building and the well laid out and tended gardens are also well visible. Best come during the week as it is very crowded at weekends.
